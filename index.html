What Is a Snow Leopard?

Snow leopards are solitary big cats native to the high mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. Known for their elusive behavior and camouflaged fur, they are rarely seen in the wild.

Habitat & Range

Snow leopards live in some of the most extreme environments on Earth, thriving at high altitudes across Central and South Asia.

Altitude

Typically found between 9,800 and 17,000 feet above sea level.

Terrain

Rocky mountains, cliffs, and steep slopes ideal for ambush hunting.

Climate

Cold, dry regions with long winters and minimal vegetation.

Geographic Range

Spans 12 countries including Nepal, China, Mongolia, and India.

Why Snow Leopards Are Endangered

  • Poaching

    Illegal hunting for fur and body parts.

  • Climate Change

    Melting glaciers and shrinking habitat.

  • Human Conflict

    Retaliation due to livestock loss.

Conservation Efforts

Organizations around the world work with local communities to protect snow leopards through tracking, education, and legal protections.
